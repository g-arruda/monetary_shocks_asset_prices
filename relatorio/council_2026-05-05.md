# Council Synthesis — `_instrucoes/` corpus (macro-paper)

*Run: 2026-05-05 — Panel: Harsh Referee (Top-5 Macro), Methodologist (Macro Time-Series), Macro Theorist (DSGE/HANK). Critic model: opus. Synthesis model: opus. Corpus: `_instrucoes/contexto.md`, `_instrucoes/Instrumento.md`, `_instrucoes/Heteroscedasticidade.md`.*

## Referee recommendation
**Major Revision.** All three critics independently arrive at Major Revision because the heteroskedasticity-identified instrument is well-motivated and the validation infrastructure is unusually thorough, but the identification chain has a load-bearing crack (A2/rank-1 on DI_2y, normalization on yield_6m, and a logically awkward JK filter applied post-GLS) that the authors document honestly without resolving. The contribution is reachable from here — none of the critics calls the design unrescuable — but the current draft cannot be accepted because the headline F=21.3 is not the structural first-stage F, and the rank-1 assumption it depends on is not formally tested.

## Identification verdict
The het-ID instrument is *partially* credible: ΔΣ has a dominant eigenvalue and rank1_share > 0.6, IBOV and BRL pass A2, and the Mertens–Ravn GLS recovery is correctly implemented. The chain breaks **first at A2 for DI_2y** (variance-ratio 99% CI [0.77, 3.53] crosses one — Methodologist and Harsh Referee converge here, Theorist concurs from a different angle), which means `b_1` is a contaminated mix rather than a pure policy column; the breakage propagates to the JK filter, which only restores power (7.6 → 21.3) by re-imposing timing-ID on a shock that was supposed to be identified by heteroskedasticity alone. **Second**, the normalization target `yield_6m` is endogenous (term-premium-bearing) and the reported F=21.3 is measured against an AR(6) of yield_6m, not against the DFM factor first-stage residual that `ident_ext_instr` actually uses — so the Stock-Yogo benchmark is being applied to the wrong regression. The instrument is plausibly informative; it is not yet shown to identify a *monetary-policy* shock under the maintained A1–A3.

## Top 3 required revisions (methodology priority)

1. **Formal rank/over-identification test for ΔΣ + confront the DI_2y A2 failure.**
   *Raised by:* Harsh Referee (#1, #3) and Methodologist (S1-#1); Theorist concurs (#2).
   *Action:* Replace the informal `rank1_share > 0.6` diagnostic with either a Cragg–Donald (1997) LR rank test or Rigobon (2003) GMM over-identification distance. Report side-by-side: (a) the 4-var SVAR with the failing A2 on DI_2y disclosed, (b) a 3-var fallback (DI_3m, IBOV, BRL) which strictly satisfies A2, and (c) `b_1` stability across the two. If the headline result requires the 4-var system, model two-factor heteroskedasticity (Sentana–Fiorentini 2001) rather than imposing rank-1 by assertion.

2. **Reconcile the headline F with the structural first-stage and fix the normalization mismatch.**
   *Raised by:* Methodologist (S1-#5, S1-#6) and Theorist (Calibration, #1); Harsh Referee touches it via "yield_6m normalization breaks Selic-rule narrative."
   *Action:* Report two Fs explicitly — the AR(p)-residualized F on the normalization variable *and* the first-stage F inside `ident_ext_instr` against the DFM monetary-factor residual (which is the F that controls weak-instrument bias in the IRF). Resolve the live mismatch where `model_alessi.R` uses `mp_var = "juros_selic"` (F≈1.1) while the docs recommend `yield_6m` (F=21.3): either (i) normalize on Selic with Anderson–Rubin confidence intervals robust to weak instruments, or (ii) provide a structural justification for yield_6m as the policy-stance normalizer (e.g., shadow-rate / forward-guidance argument) and make the code match.

3. **Justify or remove the JK filter on the GLS-recovered shock; close the monthly-aggregation exclusion gap.**
   *Raised by:* Theorist (#3, #4); Harsh Referee adjacent (#2 random-mask boundary); Methodologist (S1-#3 demean-within-regime).
   *Action:* The fact that JK *triples* F on a shock supposedly already identified by A1–A3 is diagnostic, not confirmatory — state this explicitly and either (a) drop JK and present `z_het` as the primary instrument with the lower F honestly, or (b) keep `z_het_jk` but reframe it as a hybrid het+timing identifier and re-derive its exclusion restriction. Either way, state the monthly aggregation condition E[z_het_m · η_t^j] = 0 for non-policy j, and show that Mertens–Ravn GLS does not demean within the C regime (which would absorb systematic BCB action). Run the anti-JK mask and the F(k_keep) curve already noted as open questions in `Heteroscedasticidade.md`.

## Top 3 optional revisions

1. **Sub-period instability as a formal structural-break test.**
   *Raised by:* Methodologist (S2 — Andrews 1993 QLR) and Theorist (A3 violation 2013–19 vs 2020–25); Harsh Referee notes it as "structural account needed."
   *Action:* Estimate het-ID separately on 2013–19 and 2020–25, report a Quandt–Andrews supF on `b_1`, and discuss whether 2020 is a regime change or a contamination episode rather than reporting the 3.4× F drop as a robustness margin.

2. **Bootstrap that propagates `b_1` estimation uncertainty.**
   *Raised by:* Methodologist (S1-#4) only; Theorist's GE chain implicitly depends on it.
   *Action:* Adopt Piffer–Podstawski (2018, *JEEA*) wild bootstrap that resamples within the het-ID step, not just the DFM residuals, so the IRF bands reflect uncertainty in the identified column. Current Kilian-corrected wild bootstrap is faithful to AK 2019 but understates uncertainty for proxy-SVAR-with-het-ID.

3. **IRF section + comparison to Brazilian monetary-transmission literature.**
   *Raised by:* Harsh Referee (#4) primarily; Theorist's GE concern (#4) requires it.
   *Action:* Produce the full IRF set with 68/90% bands for both `z_het_jk` and `z_jk_purif`, and benchmark against Minella (2003) and GRG (2025) to show the shock has the standard contractionary transmission (output ↓, prices ↓, BRL ↑). This converts a methodological paper into an empirical contribution.

## Per-critic verdict

- **Macro Theorist (DSGE/HANK)** — Major Revision; distinctive contribution: argues the JK-on-GLS step is *logically* inconsistent — if het-ID worked under A1–A3 it could not triple F — turning a robustness check into a falsification signal.

## Convergence and divergence
**Convergence (consolidate):** all three critics flag (i) rank-1 / A2 as informally tested rather than imposed, (ii) the F=21.3 headline as measured against the wrong regression, and (iii) the JK-on-het stack as suspicious in different vocabularies (Harsh = q99 boundary, Methodologist = misspecified residualization, Theorist = logical inconsistency). These are three views of the same underlying problem — the instrument's relevance comes partly from timing, not purely from heteroskedasticity — and should be addressed in a single revised identification section. **Divergence (open questions for authors):** the critics differ on the *fallback*. Harsh Referee prefers a 3-var SVAR (DI_3m, IBOV, BRL) that strictly satisfies A2; Methodologist wants a richer daily VAR(1)/VAR(2) with stable ΔΣ and Piffer–Podstawski bootstrap; Theorist wants either an Anderson–Rubin / weak-IV-robust route on Selic or a structural argument for yield_6m. The authors must pick one and defend it rather than splitting the difference.
